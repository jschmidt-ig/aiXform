Prerequisites

Fundamentals + Labs Required (Phases 1 & 2)

This phase requires completion of Fundamentals (Weeks 1-2, 20 sessions) and Labs (Weeks 3-6, 4 hands-on labs). Fundamentals builds the mental model for LLMs, context engineering, agents, and security. Labs develops hands-on skills in safe environments. Applied brings it all together on real work.

Choosing Focus Areas

Select the right projects for Applied phase to maximize learning and minimize risk.

Good Focus Areas

  • Add test coverage to an under-tested module
  • Refactor a well-understood but messy component
  • Generate/update documentation for a service
  • Implement a small, well-defined feature
  • Fix a backlog of minor bugs
  • Bounded scope with visible impact

Avoid These

  • Critical path production changes
  • Security-sensitive code without proper review
  • Large architectural changes
  • Anything requiring extensive approvals
  • High-risk if something goes wrong
  • Areas where team lacks merge authority

The Coaching Model

Facilitation-first approach that builds team ownership and sustainability.

Coach as Facilitator

The coach's role is to facilitate, not prescribe. We help teams discover what works for them, ask questions more than give answers, and share patterns from other teams (anonymized). Creating space for experimentation is more valuable than dictating solutions.

Team Ownership

Teams own their playbook content, workflow decisions, success metrics, and adoption pace. This isn't a one-size-fits-all program - it's a framework for discovering what works in your specific context.

Safe Experimentation

We encourage trying new approaches, celebrate learning from failures, and maintain no judgment on "wrong" approaches. The focus is on continuous improvement, not perfection from day one.
