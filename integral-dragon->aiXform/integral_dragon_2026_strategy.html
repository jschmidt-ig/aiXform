2026 Strategy: stabilize revenue, fix delivery, productize training — then build software.

The 2026 plan is not “become a product company overnight.” It is a controlled shift from hourly services to predictable delivery and repeatable offerings, with AI SDLC Training as the first product. This creates runway resilience and compounding IP without breaking current consulting revenue.

Recommendation-first Training-as-product wedge Stability before scale

Top recommendations

  1. Fix the delivery operating model (capacity, backlog, engagement shapes).
  2. Launch AI SDLC Training as a product (repeatable, outcomes-focused).
  3. Move hourly → retainers to reduce concentration risk + stabilize utilization.
Non-negotiable principle: productization fails if delivery is chaotic. Fix the factory first.

Core strategy (Rumelt kernel)

This is the “one page” strategy logic: what’s true, the rules we’ll follow, and what we’ll do.
DIAGNOSIS

What’s true right now

The current constraints are not abstract. They are operational, and they block productization.

  • Revenue concentration risk: losing one contract is existential.
  • Delivery doesn’t scale: reactive utilization and high context switching.
  • No protected capacity: product/training loses to billable work every time.
  • Demand signal exists: AI SDLC training is sellable now.
GUIDING POLICY

Decision rules we enforce

These are the rules that force the “move right” without pretending we’re already a product company.

  • Stability before scale: optimize for 3–6 month survivability.
  • Delivery system first: fix flow before adding ambition.
  • Training is first product: not software (yet).
  • Explicit capacity allocation: protected time, not leftovers.
  • Every engagement compounds: reusable assets every sprint.
COHERENT ACTIONS

What we will do (concrete)

Actions that directly solve the diagnosis while building leverage.

  • Move hourly → capacity-based retainers for predictability.
  • Standardize engagement shapes (Diagnostic → Pilot → Scale/Enable).
  • Package AI SDLC training with tiers + outcomes.
  • Implement a minimal delivery operating model (capacity, backlog, cadence).

Recommendations (what to do first)

These are ordered. If you do #2 or #3 without #1, execution degrades.
1

Fix the delivery operating model

Delivery and utilization are the current bottleneck. Product work will not stick without predictable flow.

Do: one backlog, explicit capacity, standard engagement shapes, weekly cadence.
2

Launch AI SDLC training as a product

Training is the fastest path to leverage: reusable IP, easier sales motion, and it feeds consulting.

Do: package outcomes, define tiers, ship curriculum v1, pilot with one client.
3

Move hourly → retainers for stability

Stability goal is survivability (3–6 months), not maximal growth. Retainers reduce churn and volatility.

Do: capacity packages, 3+ month terms, explicit scopes, renewal cadence.

Minimum viable delivery operating model

This is the smallest set of process changes that unlock predictable utilization and protected product time.
NON-NEGOTIABLES

What changes

  • Explicit work types: Client Delivery • Product/Training • Sales/Ops.
  • Intentional capacity split: example 70% Delivery • 20% Product/Training • 10% Ops/Sales.
  • Standard engagement shapes: Diagnostic → Pilot → Scale/Enable.
  • Single prioritized backlog: one queue, explicit trade-offs, no hidden work.
  • Cadence: weekly plan/review, biweekly artifact release, monthly exec outcomes.

Rule: if something violates the capacity split or bypasses the backlog, it doesn’t happen (or it’s a deliberate exception approved by leadership).

OUTCOMES

What it enables

  • Schedulable delivery: fewer surprises and missed deadlines.
  • Protected product time: training and IP actually ship.
  • Lower context switching: better quality and faster cycle time.
  • Predictable revenue: easier to grow without operational burnout.

Reality check: this is not bureaucracy. This is the minimum structure required to scale beyond heroics.

AI SDLC training as the first product

Position training as outcomes + repeatable delivery, with optional coaching. This is your wedge.
TIER 1 • CORE COURSE

Baseline capability

Standard curriculum with repeatable labs and clear outcomes. Designed to be delivered frequently.

  • Audience: dev teams, tech leads, architects
  • Outcomes: baseline workflows, guardrails, patterns
  • Includes: curriculum, labs, templates, scorecards
TIER 2 • ENTERPRISE VARIANT

Contextualized for the org

Same core, customized to tooling, governance, and real internal reference problems.

  • Audience: platform teams, security, leadership
  • Outcomes: org-specific patterns + governance alignment
  • Includes: tailored labs, policy mapping, adoption plan
TIER 3 • ENABLEMENT + COACHING

Adoption and sustained change

Coaching turns training into behavior change: office hours, reviews, and reinforcement.

  • Audience: teams shipping production work
  • Outcomes: durable adoption, improved throughput
  • Includes: office hours, workflow reviews, Q&A
What “productize training” means (not fluff)

Productizing training means it behaves like a product operationally: defined scope, repeatable delivery, measurable outcomes, consistent materials, and predictable pricing tiers.

  • Sell outcomes: time saved, fewer defects, faster delivery cycles, safer AI usage.
  • Ship curriculum versions: v1 → v1.1 → v2, with changelogs and reusable labs.
  • Build a delivery kit: slide decks, lab repos, rubrics, checklists, facilitator notes.
  • Make it easy to buy: clear packages and add-ons, not custom proposals every time.

Revenue stability goal: 3–6 months survivability

The 2026 priority is resilience: don’t be one contract away from a crisis.
CURRENT RISK

Concentration risk

If one major contract drops, the organization is destabilized immediately.

  • Hiring and product work get paused
  • Utilization swings wildly
  • Delivery and quality degrade under stress
TARGET STATE

Portfolio resiliency

Maintain a portfolio that can survive the loss of any single client relationship.

  • 2–4 core contracts with 3+ month terms
  • Mix revenue streams: consulting retainers + training deliveries + advisory
  • Renewal cadence: quarterly renewals with outcome reviews

Training product Fixed curriculum + tiers + outcomes, delivered repeatedly. Reusable IP compounds; diversifies revenue; builds authority.
